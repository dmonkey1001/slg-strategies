Texas Cyber Command

A Strategic Blueprint for a Resilient Texas

A "Whole-of-Ecosystem" Defense

The Texas Cyber Command (TCC) was established by House Bill 150 to serve as the state's centralized authority for cybersecurity. Its mission is to defend against cyber threats, lead coordinated incident response, and build a resilient digital future for all Texas public entities by integrating state, local, academic, and federal partnerships.

Phased Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (0-18 Mo)

Establish leadership and core staff. Procure and deploy core SIEM & EDR tech. Launch 24/7 Cyber Threat Intelligence Center (CTIC).

Phase 2: Expansion (18-36 Mo)

Roll out services to local governments. Deploy regional Field Remediation Teams. Implement SOAR for automation and launch cost-recovery services.

Phase 3: FOC (36+ Mo)

Achieve Full Operational Capability. Implement proactive threat hunting. Deepen critical infrastructure partnerships and establish national leadership.
